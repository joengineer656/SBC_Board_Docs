The board's full mechanical geometry is available as open-source CAD files. Use these files to design a custom enclosure, integrate the board into a larger assembly, verify clearances against existing hardware, or 3D print a mounting bracket — everything you need is available for free download.

## Designing an Enclosure

### Key Design Considerations

When designing a custom enclosure, keep the following in mind:

- **Connector positions:** The USB-C power port, USB Type-A ports, HDMI connector, Ethernet jack, and 3.5mm audio jack all sit on the board edges. Route cutouts precisely — use the STEP model to confirm exact positions before finalising your enclosure geometry.
- **Ventilation:** The SoC and power components generate heat during sustained workloads. Include ventilation slots or a fan mount above the processor area to prevent thermal throttling. A minimum of 10 mm clearance above the tallest component is recommended.
- **Mounting hole standoffs:** The four mounting holes accept M2.5 screws. Use standoffs tall enough to clear the solder joints on the underside of the board (typically 3–5 mm).

### Community Enclosures

Before designing from scratch, check whether the community has already built something that fits your needs. Search for enclosures on [Printables](https://www.printables.com) and [Thingiverse](https://www.thingiverse.com) using the board name — you will often find fully parametric models ready to customise and print.

### Importing the STEP File

<Tabs>
  <Tab title="FreeCAD">
    <Steps>
      <Step title="Open FreeCAD">
        Launch FreeCAD and select **File → Open**.
      </Step>
      <Step title="Select the STEP file">
        Navigate to the downloaded `.step` file and click **Open**. FreeCAD automatically imports it using the built-in STEP importer.
      </Step>
      <Step title="Switch to Part workbench">
        Use the **Workbench** dropdown to select **Part**. You can now inspect, measure, and use the board model as a reference body when building your enclosure around it.
      </Step>
      <Step title="Apply a Boolean operation">
        Create your enclosure body in the **Part Design** workbench, then use **Part → Boolean → Cut** to subtract the board volume from the enclosure shell, ensuring a precise fit.
      </Step>
    </Steps>
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="Fusion 360">
    <Steps>
      <Step title="Open a new design">
        In Fusion 360, create a new design or open an existing enclosure project.
      </Step>
      <Step title="Insert the STEP file">
        Go to **Insert → Insert Mesh** or **Insert → Import** and select the `.step` file. Fusion 360 imports it as a solid body.
      </Step>
      <Step title="Set the origin">
        Align the board's corner or a mounting hole to your design origin using **Modify → Align** to ensure consistent positioning relative to your enclosure geometry.
      </Step>
      <Step title="Design around the board">
        With the board body in place, sketch your enclosure walls, cutouts, and mounting bosses directly over it. Use the **Inspect → Measure** tool to confirm clearances.
      </Step>
    </Steps>
  </Tab>
</Tabs>

<Tip>
  Use **M2.5 brass threaded standoffs** when mounting the board inside an enclosure. Brass standoffs resist corrosion, provide a secure ground connection through the mounting holes, and are less likely to crack under torque than plastic equivalents. A standard M2.5 × 5 mm standoff with an M2.5 × 6 mm screw gives a solid fit without placing stress on the PCB.
</Tip>
